Zone-Era Concepts are the older cluster of ideas around zones, [[DPT]]s, [[ZLM]]s, [[ZTM]]s, and metric-space
classification.
They are superseded as primary ontology authority, but they remain signal-bearing.

Current owner-answer-informed critique:
The core mistake was treating zones as a separate world authority when [[Phenomenon]] can already be localized,
distributed, point-like, or region-like.
A region-like phenomenon can do much of the useful work that a zone was meant to do without introducing a separate
zone authority layer.

Reusable pressure:
Zone-era mechanisms remain useful as intermediate-layer classification, authoring, detail-generation, or
realization-selection tools.
The mistake was treating zones as world authority rather than glue between metrics, phenomena, chunks, and
materialization/re-aggregation.

Current owner-answer-informed terms:

- `DPT` remains a useful name for Data Point n-Tensor.
- `ZLM` remains a useful name for Zone Lookup Map: a scale-local map from metric-space combinations into zone/type
  classifications, with full coverage or explicit fallback handling.
- `ZTM` remains grouped with this cluster until it gets a dedicated pressure pass.
- Zone-like patterns may continue to exist inside phenomenon-owned logic or intermediate classification layers without
  becoming standalone ontology authority.

Classification boundary:
A [[Phenomenon]] is itself a classification region in the current model.
This means classification regions are not dead, but they should usually be understood through phenomena or
intermediate metric-space tooling rather than as independent chunk-owned ontology.

Do not treat old zone docs as final target architecture.
Treat them as superseded-but-signal-bearing until the replacement vocabulary is fully pressure-tested.
